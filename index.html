What is ASMR ?


Autonomous Sensory Meridian Response

What is ASMR?

ASMR is a tingly feeling that travels from the top of the scalp down the back of the neck to the upper spine. It’s triggered by specific auditory or visual stimuli, such as whispering, tapping, and natural sounds.

Positive Responses

Watching videos that trigger ASMR is thought to lead to positive responses throughout the body, including enhanced mood, relaxation, and even pain relief.

Stress and Anxiety Reduction

There is clear evidence that ASMR can reduce stress and anxiety. This can be particularly beneficial during work or study sessions, as it can help individuals stay focused and motivated.

Improvement in Short-Term Memory

Data shows that ASMR can improve short-term memory. This can enhance learning and information retention during study sessions.

Enhanced Productivity

Listening to ASMR can boost productivity noticeably. One experiment showed a work efficiency increase of 22% when listening to ASMR during work.

Improved Concentration

ASMR helps the mind enter a meditative state by paying attention to gentle stimuli, which can lead to increased focus and concentration.

Cognitive Function Boost

ASMR sound effects can help boost brain abilities and other cognitive functions.

Relaxation and Well-being

The tingling sensation running from the scalp down the spine provides a sense of relaxation and well-being.
